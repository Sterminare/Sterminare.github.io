Hector the Hexapod

Hector is a 12DOF, six-legged hexapod robot. It can walk, spin in place, and detect obstacles in front of it. It is controlled wirelessly by an android app.

It is made of PLA plastic parts made on my 3D printers.

Designed by Naus at Thingiverse

Electronic and Control

The robot is controlled by a droidscript application on a smartphone, communicating with the ESP32 microcontroller over wifi. The ESP32 controls a PCA9685 16-channel PWM controller which powers the 12 SG90 servos. Hector also has a HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor to see obstacles in front of it. Everything is powered by two 18650 LiPo cells that can be removed and recharged externally. The ESP32 is programmed using the Arduino IDE.
