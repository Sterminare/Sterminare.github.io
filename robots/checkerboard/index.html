Renders

This is a robotic checkerboard, that uses magnets and a mechanised gantry underneath to move around its pieces, while using hall-effect sensors inside the board to detect the human player's pieces and decide its next move.

The play area is the size of a standard checker/chessboard - 16x16in (2x2in squares), and the frame itself is not much larger at ~19x19in. The frame is made of 15mm aluminum T-slot extrusions. The board is made of two sheets of 3/16in thick hardboard, with a 1/8in gap between, where the 32 hall effect sensors are located.

The Board

All 32 hall-effect sensors are latching and are hand soldered in place with three wires and a resistor. The board slides into the slots of the aluminum frame for a very secure mount.

More

The mechanism uses these sliders that slide in the slots of the aluminum extrusions. They will be driven by two stepper motors.

Pieces

The pieces are all 3D-printed, with magnets inside that enable them to be moved by the mechanism below and to be detected by the hall effect sensors. To king pieces, they will not be flipped over, because this would disable the hall effect sensors. Instead, they will have detachable pieces that magnetically attach on top to indicate that they are 'kinged'.
